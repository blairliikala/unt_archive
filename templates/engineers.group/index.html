Recording & Streaming Checklist

Pre-Show

  1. Clock In.
  2. Enter Program into ExpressionEngine.
  3. Open Logic, create folders on desktop and recall preset. How?
  4. Adjust mic preamp gain levels. Preamps are bookedmarked in Chrome, Grace m108.
    Most gain amount is between 30-40. Percussin should be 25, classical guitar 40.
  5. Open live streaming page and Helo to verify stream starts correctly.

Start of Concert

  1. Frame-up video camera shot.
  2. Start recording in Logic.

Note Denon's will automatically start. This is the backup recorder.

End of Concert

  1. Change ExpressionEngine entry from Scheduled to Pending.
    This should shut down the livestream, and stop the video encoders within 1min or less.
  2. Stop recording in Logic.

Note the Denon's will continue recording till the end of the recital timeslot.

Post Production

Audio (Edit in Logic as normal)

  1. Organize your project folder. Abbreviations
  2. No Special Characters in filenames.wav, like accent marks or double spaces.

After content is uploaded:

  1. When finished, Change from Pending to Waiting for Review.
  2. Cut and paste command ⌘+drag project to "Filestore"
    /music.nas.unt.edu/comstreaming/Audio Archive Raw/[current semester]/
  3. Quit all open applications cmd+Q

When Leaving

  1. Turn speakers down to zero.
  2. COVID: try to wipe down the keyboard and area.
  3. Turn Off: Any Hall TV monitors
  4. Turn Off: Soundcraft console
  5. Turn Off: Lights
  6. Computers stay powered on
  7. CLOCK OUT

General Policies

This department is a Professional Service, not a classroom.

  • Missing a concert is an immediate termination unless an understandable excuse exists that I can tell the administration.
  • If you wish to make alt. mixes with automation...etc... we can not pay you beyond what is asked for archiving, but you are allowed to create them off the clock during off-peak hours.
  • CoM Project files can not leave University hard drives. You can make a copy of the final mix-down, but not track-breakouts.
  • No student/personal projects on University drives. Bring your own, off-peak time only.
  • Gear: I need to approve anything not RS-related. Borrowing without approval is stealing.
  • Do not install programs, software, or software update.
  • Do not re-wire anything.
  • Computer drives are not a safe location for any data long-term.
  • Set Correct Expectations:
    - Don't promise anything to faculty or students. Say "Let me check" or "I will have to talk with Blair." "probably, maybe, and usually" are close enough to YES and are not acceptible.
  • Food and Drinks stay away from equipment.
  • Recordings should be completed the night of, or next morning, and posted online within 48 hours. We tell performers and the public "within the week."
